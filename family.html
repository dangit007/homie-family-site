Family

The week belongs to everyone. The controls stay with you.

Kids, grandparents, and the nanny can all see the plan on their own phones. What each person can see and do is up to you.

Kids

Maya checks her own week.

Maya is 11. On her own account, she sees her week: where she has to be, when she leaves, and who is picking her up. No more asking Mom the same question three times a night.

And because her account only sees, she can’t move practice or delete her brother’s dentist appointment. The schedule stays safe.

Helpers

The nanny runs her day from it.

The nanny’s account works the same way, with one difference: she can contribute. She sees her days and every handoff, and when the teacher mentions a field trip at pickup, she can add it as a draft for you to review.

Grandparents and other helpers join the same way. Everyone who helps run the week can finally see it.

Access

You choose what each person can see and do.

Every family member is a person in Homie, not a setting. Tap someone to choose their access, send their invite, or change what they can do. Kids can’t sign up on their own; a child’s account exists only because you created the invite.

Access is enforced where the data lives, not just hidden in the app. A view-only account can’t change the schedule from any phone.
