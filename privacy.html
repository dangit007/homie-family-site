Privacy

Your family’s week is nobody’s business.

Where your children are, hour by hour, is not data to feed an ad network. Homie is built by a parent, and it looks after your family’s week the way he looks after his own.

Voice stays on your phone

When you dictate a plan, the words are turned into text right on your device. The audio recording is never uploaded, and it’s never saved anywhere else.

No ads. No trackers. Ever.

No ads. No analytics tools quietly watching in the background. Homie makes no money from knowing your schedule, so there’s no reason to gather more than it needs.

Only your family can read it

Your household’s plans are stored so that only your signed-in family members can open them. They’re locked to your household with row-level security, so no other family using Homie can read your week.

AI that forgets

When smarter capture is on, only the words you typed or dictated are sent to be understood, then passed straight back. They’re never stored, and never used to train anything. And it’s optional: Homie works without AI at all.

Kids join by invite only

A child can’t sign themselves up. An account for your kid exists only because you created the invite, and you decide what it can see and do.

Your phone holds the plan

Homie works offline first, so your own phone always has the plan. It syncs to your family’s other phones, and nowhere else.
